📋 Cookie Policy — The Shortbread Manifesto

Last Updated Effective date: 2025-05-05 · No changes ever made to track users

Azure Plate Diary is built on a radical privacy architecture: no cookies, no session identifiers, no fingerprinting, no invisible trackers. We believe digital privacy should be as pure as an untouched cream velouté. This Cookies Policy explains the absence of any tracking technology on our domain and how we comply with ePrivacy Directive (EU Cookie Law), GDPR, CCPA, and global privacy standards.

🧈 Creamy clarity: Our website does NOT utilize any HTTP cookies, web storage (localStorage/sessionStorage), IndexedDB, cache-based identifiers, or browser fingerprinting techniques. You won't see a cookie banner because there is simply nothing to disclose — we never plant digital crumbs.

🚫 Absolutely No Tracking Cookies — Ever

What we never deploy

  • Essential cookies: Our site doesn't require login, shopping carts, or user preferences — so no session cookies.
  • Analytics cookies: No Google Analytics, Matomo, Plausible, or any analytics scripts. We serve raw static assets.
  • Marketing / Advertising cookies: Zero ad networks, retargeting pixels, or affiliate trackers. No Facebook, no LinkedIn Insights.
  • Functional preferences: Since we don't store theme preferences or UI customizations, no cookie is needed.
  • Third-party cookies: Our pages host no external widgets (YouTube, Vimeo, Twitter embeds) — every resource is self-hosted.

Why you'll never see a cookie banner

Under the ePrivacy Directive, cookie consent banners are required only for non-essential cookies. Since Azure Plate Diary sets zero cookies of any kind, a banner would be misleading. We respect your consent by design — not by pop-ups. Even “implied consent” mechanisms are irrelevant here. Our codebase contains no cookie-writing functions whatsoever.

🍪 "Digital cookies" in our pantry = zero. Real cream shortbread cookies in our recipe section = plenty. Feel free to bake our Triple Cream Shortbread — but your browser will remain crumb-free.

🔍 Local Storage & Client-Side Data — None Used for Tracking

We do not store any persistent data on your device. Our HTML/CSS/JS is minimal and interactive only for smooth scrolling, recipe toggles, and contact form preview simulation. No values are written to localStorage, sessionStorage, or any browser database. Any client-side state (e.g., opening a recipe accordion) is purely ephemeral and disappears on page refresh. We don't fingerprint your browser, cache identifiers, or use ETag tracking techniques.

Technical verification: You can open your browser's developer tools (Application/Storage tab) while visiting azureplatediary.com — you'll find zero cookies, empty localStorage, empty sessionStorage. No surprises. We encourage digital hygiene.

📡 Third-Party Resources & Embedded Content — A Clean Kitchen Policy

Unlike most recipe websites that embed Google Fonts, YouTube how-to videos, or social share buttons, Azure Plate Diary is fully self-contained. All fonts are system default stacks (Inter, Playfair Display, Segoe UI). Icons are inline SVGs or text emojis. No external CDNs for tracking or content delivery networks that set cookies. This ensures that no external domain can drop third-party cookies on our visitors.

We explicitly avoid:

  • ❌ Google Fonts / Adobe Fonts (they can log IPs)
  • ❌ Embedded YouTube or Vimeo players (they set tracking cookies)
  • ❌ Social media sharing buttons (Twitter, FB often embed pixels)
  • ❌ Disqus or any comment system
  • ❌ Captcha services (reCAPTCHA uses cookies)

The only external links are manual hyperlinks to authoritative cream resources (academic papers, historical dairy archives) but these open in a new tab and do not set cookies on our domain — refer to those sites' own policies.

⚙️ Server Logs & Anonymized Requests — No Personal Cookie Data

Our hosting provider retains minimal server logs for security and troubleshooting (request URI, timestamp, HTTP status). These logs do not include cookie data, referrer strings that identify user-specific identifiers, nor do they correlate visits using any cookie-like mechanism. IP addresses are anonymized at the infrastructure level (last octet removed). Logs are rotated every 24 hours and never mined for behavioral analysis. In short: server logs contain no information that could be linked to a cookie or cross-session tracking.

🛡️ Defense-in-depth: Because we set no cookies, there is zero risk of “cookie syncing” or “cookie matching” with third-party ad tech. Your cream exploration remains entirely anonymous.

📜 Cookie Compliance & Legal Bases (GDPR / ePrivacy / CCPA)

ePrivacy Directive (Cookie Law): Article 5(3) requires consent for storing information on a user's terminal. Since we store no information, no consent is required. Our website is fully compliant without banners.

GDPR (EU): Processing of personal data requires a legal basis. We don't process any personal data via cookies or trackers. Therefore, GDPR compliance is inherent. The only potential personal data would be voluntary email contact (handled separately via privacy policy).

CCPA (California): We do not sell personal information. The absence of cookies means no “sale” of cookie data, no sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising. California residents have the right to know, delete, and opt-out — but we have nothing to disclose.

Brazil LGPD & other frameworks: Same principle: no tracking, no cookies, no worries.

If you’d like a formal “Do Not Track” assurance, our servers ignore DNT headers because the entire site is stateless and cookie-free. We also respect Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals by default — there are no trackers to disable.

🍦 Frequently Asked “Crumb” Questions

Does Azure Plate Diary use any “first-party” analytics cookies?

Absolutely not. No cookies, period. We do not use any analytics platform that sets cookies. Our visitor count is unknown by design — we prioritize privacy over vanity metrics.

What about “strictly necessary” cookies for security?

Our infrastructure does not require login, CSRF tokens, or session identifiers. Therefore no necessary cookies exist. If we ever added a password-protected area (unlikely), we would explicitly disclose it.

Does the contact form store cookie data?

No. Our contact form is HTML/CSS with JavaScript for front-end simulation (no data sent to a server on the live demo). Even in a production configuration, the email transmission would happen without writing any cookies or local storage.

Can I still enjoy the website if I block all cookies?

Yes! Since we set zero cookies, any browser privacy setting (block all cookies, isolate storage, use incognito) will not affect your experience. Every recipe, cream guide, and rhapsody loads perfectly.
